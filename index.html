Pune Smart City

During my internship at McKinsey & Co., I had the opportunity to work as a consultant for the Pune Municipal Corporation's Smart City project. In the Indian context, the National Smart Cities Mission is a government initiative aimed at revitalizing and modernizing urban areas across the country, with a focus on enhancing citizen-friendliness and sustainability.
My specific role within the project was to oversee the implementation of "Smart Elements" in Pune. This involved managing various smart features integrated into the city, such as Variable Message Display (VMD) screens, flood sensors, emergency call boxes, and Wi-Fi stations. Ensuring effective coordination and communication among stakeholders was a key aspect of my responsibilities, as it was crucial for the seamless deployment of these elements.
To guarantee compliance with project specifications, I conducted regular site visits and generated comprehensive reports. By closely monitoring the deployment process, I could verify that the implementation was carried out as intended and address any issues or discrepancies that arose.
Working on the Smart Elements aspect of the Pune Smart City project allowed me to contribute to the realization of a more technologically advanced and sustainable urban environment. It was a valuable experience that deepened my understanding of project coordination and reinforced the importance of effective stakeholder management in large-scale initiatives.

Smart City Projects: Karnataka

1. MyCity: Smart City Forum

MyCity - The Smart City Forum project serves as a platform to promote transparency and citizen engagement in local governments' public works. Built on the principles of open data and citizen participation, it facilitates effective communication between governing officers and the community they serve.
Through this platform, governing officers can upload comprehensive details about ongoing projects in the city, including contractor information, allocated funds, and timelines. This commitment to transparency ensures that citizens have access to complete information about each project directly from the government. Citizens, in turn, can view and review these listed works, offering feedback and updates by leaving comments and adding relevant pictures on the portal. Furthermore, they have the option to "subscribe and follow" specific projects of interest, enabling them to receive timely updates through SMS and email notifications. By actively involving citizens in the project monitoring process, their feedback becomes an invaluable resource that closes the loop and helps enhance the overall utility of the initiative.
